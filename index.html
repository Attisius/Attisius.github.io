About

Hello!

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute for Data, Econometrics, Algorithms, and Learning (IDEAL) in Chicago, where I am hosted by Lev Reyzin (University of Illinois Chicago), Avrim Blum, and Nati Srebro (Toyota Technological Institute at Chicago).

I earned my PhD from the Computer Science Department at Ben-Gurion University, where I had the privilege of being advised by Aryeh Kontorovich (Ben-Gurion University) and Yishay Mansour (Tel Aviv University and Google Research). Prior to that, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science at Tel Aviv University.

I’ll be joining the Computer Science Department at the Technion as a faculty member in 2027.
Feel free to reach out to chat about research and opportunities.

Research interests

My primary research interests lie in the foundations of machine learning theory and data-driven sequential decision-making, as well as their intersections with game theory, private data analysis, information theory, signal processing, theoretical computer science and cognitive science.
